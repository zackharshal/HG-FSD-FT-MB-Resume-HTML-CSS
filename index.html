  • Blind Stick

    The "Blind Stick" project is an innovative solution designed to assist visually impaired individuals in navigating their surroundings with greater safety and independence. It incorporates various components, including an MSP430 microcontroller, an ultrasonic sensor, a buzzer, and a Bluetooth module, to create a functional and user-friendly device. The primary function of the Blind Stick is to detect obstacles in the user's path and provide real-time feedback. Additionally, the Blind Stick integrates a Bluetooth module, enabling connectivity with a smartphone. A dedicated mobile application is developed as part of the project, allowing the blind user to establish a connection between the stick and the smartphone. Through the app, the user can access various features and functionalities.


  • The Connect Four Game

    The project "Connect 4" that was developed using core Java. This application was designed to provide a digital platform for users to enjoy the classic two-player game, Connect 4. The Connect 4 project offers a user-friendly interface, allowing players to download and easily engage with the game. The game follows the same logic and rules as the traditional Connect 4 game. The objective is for players to strategically drop colored discs into a vertical grid, aiming to connect four of their discs in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal line. The first player to achieve this alignment is declared the winner.


  • LIFI

    The "LiFi" project is an innovative endeavor that leverages the power of light waves to transmit data wirelessly. Unlike traditional wireless communication methods that rely on radio frequency signals, LiFi utilizes light-emitting diodes (LEDs) to transmit data. The project consists of two key circuits: the transmitting circuit and the receiving circuit. The transmitting circuit is responsible for encoding the data and modulating it onto the light emitted by the LED. As the LED emits light, the encoded data is transmitted through the light waves. On the receiving end, the receiving circuit captures the light waves using a solar panel. The solar panel converts the received light signals back into electrical signals, which are then processed by the circuit. The processed data is then made available for further use, such as playback through a speaker
